Hello World. My name is Chalence Safranek-Shrader. I grew up in sunny Los Angeles, California, and received my B.S. in astrophysics from UCLA in 2007. In 2014 I was awarded my Ph.D from the University of Texas at Austin where I was jointly advised by Volker Bromm and Milos Milosavljevic. I am currently a postdoctoral fellow at the University of California, Berkeley where I work with Christopher McKee and Richard Klein.

Research wise, I'm interested in understanding how stars form across cosmic time - both at high redshifts and at the present day. I tackle this by means of large-scale radiation-magneto-hydrodynamics simulations which follow the progression of star formation from large (cosmological Mpc, or galactic disk Kpc) scales down to those of protostellar disks (tens of AU).

Data wise, I'm keen to get my hands dirty.

In my ample spare time, I enjoy cooking, rock climbing, jazz piano, volleyball, food infomercials, Skymall (RIP), tennis, and writing brief autobiographical statements for use on my website.
