Hello World. My name is Chalence Safranek-Shrader. I grew up in sunny Los Angeles, California, and received my B.S. in astrophysics from UCLA in 2007. In 2014 I was awarded my Ph.D from the University of Texas at Austin where I was jointly advised by Volker Bromm and Milos Milosavljevic. I am currently a postdoctoral fellow at the University of California, Berkeley where I work with Christopher McKee and Richard Klein.

Research wise, I'm interested in understanding how stars form across cosmic time - both at high redshifts and at the present day. I tackle this by means of large-scale radiation-magneto-hydrodynamic simulations which follow the progression of star formation from large (Mpc/Kpc) scales down to those of protostellar disks (tens of AU).

When not looking at a computer screen, I enjoy cooking, rock climbing, jazz piano, volleyball, food infomercials, Skymall, tennis, and writing brief autobiographical statements for use on my website.
